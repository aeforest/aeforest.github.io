Roguelike Game - CS 229

The main component of CS 229 was a semester-long individual project in C and C++. I developed a roguelike game by implementing a new component each week. Censure - CS 309

CS 309 was a semester-long project. I worked on a team of 4 members. I worked primarily on our MySQL database and writing PHP code. For this project, we created a web application. This application allowed users to login to their social media websites such as Twitter and Facebook and then scan through their past posts. Lexer Language - CS 319

Since there were only 2 people, we did not think specific team roles were neccessary, so we made sure to communicate frequently to ensure that everything got done. For the final portfolio, my partner and I created a programming language using ANTLR. Our language was capable of handling boolean and numerical variables, if/else statements, and method calls. This also required some Java coding and the Java BeanShell library.

Accu-Pop - SE 491 (Senior Design)

Just this semester, Spring 2016, I started on my senior design project. I the team leader for our team of 6 students. We are currently still in the planning and initial experimentation phase. We will be creating a device for catchers and second basemen to wear during a baseball scouting event. This device will be used to measure the catcher's pop-time, which is the amount of time it takes for the catcher to get the ball to the second basemen after he catches the pitch.

General Education Reflection

GE1. What are your current short term (within next one or two years) and long term (next five to ten years) goals? You may include your ideal career in engineering or your preferred working environment.

As I am approaching graduation, I have come up with a few goals that I would like to accomplish in the near future. Obviously, one of those goals is to find a great job. Before I can do that however, I have to figure our what a great job even looks like. To help figure that out, I've had internships and talked to people with careers at various companies. I still have a lot to figure out, but I have learned a few things so far.

One of the easiest is location. I know many people who have big plans to get as far away from the state of Iowa as fast as possible once they graduate. I however would like to stay. I've worked in Des Moines for a summer, and I'll be working in Ames this summer, and I learned that anyone who thinks the only good jobs are on a coast is wrong.

I have also learned that different companies have different cultures, and finding an enjoyable culture is very important. So far, I've found that other people I've talked to feel more motivated when working in an innovative and competitive environment. I can see myself enjoying that type of culture as well.

In addition to starting my career shortly after graduation, I have some long term goals. Once I find the right company for me, I would really like to stay at that company long-term and get to know what they do. I have noticed that it is easy to fall into a routine at work where I focus only on my job and ignore everything going on around me. I think it would be much more enjoyable if I took the time to see what other things are happening at the company and how my work fits into the big picture.

GE2. What have you learned in your general education electives that allow you to evaluate and formulate engineering solutions with problem solving and innovation beyond the technical aspects in problem solving? How do general education classes help you to think about an engineering problem?

I think that the main skill I refined through my general education courses was communication, which is very important. An engineer must be able to communicate the technical plans and progress of their work, and I believe that Iowa State has prepared me to do that.

I have also worked towards a minor in mathematics, which I find to be very helpful when solving technical problems. I find myself using math when trying to figure out which algorithm will be more efficient for a programming project, or other things like that. I also think that my math background helps make the complex topics in my upper level computer science and software engineering courses easier to understand.

GE3. Describe specific instances where you used knowledge/skills from general elective courses for innovation in engineering problems. What impact does meshing technical engineering skills and general electives-based innovation have in an economic, global, or societal context?

Anytime I work on a team project, I am meshing my technical skills and my general skills. If everyone on the team had great technical skills but couldn't communicate effectively, the project would probably not progress very efficiently. There would be situations where 2 people did the same work, or maybe 2 people would have different, clashing ideas about how to solve the same problem. Communication is necessary for a team to operate smoothly.

As the team leader for my senior design project, I have a responsibility to communicate with my team, our stakeholders, and our advisor to make sure we are progressing at an acceptable speed and doing quality work.

CR6. In the context of the first four questions, if you were to do your undergraduate work again, what things would you change?

I waited until this semester to do a lot of my technical electives, and now I am at the point where I don't have a lot of flexibility in my schedule to choose which electives I want to take. If I had looked into this earlier and started finding technical electives that looked especially interesting, I might have been able to have more input into what I took.
