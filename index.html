Roguelike Game - CS 229

The main component of CS 229 was a semester-long individual project in C and C++. We developed a roguelike game by implementing a new component each week. Tasks at the beginning consisted of things like generating a random map with a certain number of rooms and tunnels that connect them all. Towards the end, we wrote code to simulate different types of monsters that would spawn into the game and have different stats.

Censure - CS 309

CS 309 was a semester-long project. I worked on a team of 4 members. I worked primarily on our MySQL database and writing PHP code. For this project, we created a web application. This application allowed users to login to their social media websites such as Twitter and Facebook and then scan through their past posts. Our app would scan for swearing or other potetially unprofessional posts and show these posts to the user. The user could then decide if they wanted to delete those posts or not.

Lexer Language - CS 319

In CS 319, we had several portfolio projects throughout the semester that we completed in groups of 2. For the final portfolio, my partner and I created a programming language using ANTLR. Our language was capable of handling boolean and numerical variables, if/else statements, and method calls. This also required some Java coding and the Java BeanShell library.

Accu-Pop - SE 491 (Senior Design)

Just this semester, Spring 2016, I started on my senior design project. I the team leader for our team of 6 students. We are currently still in the planning and initial experimentation phase. We will be creating a device for catchers and second basemen to wear during a baseball scouting event. This device will be used to measure the catcher's pop-time, which is the amount of time it takes for the catcher to get the ball to the second basemen after he catches the pitch.
